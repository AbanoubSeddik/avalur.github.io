Oral Tradition
(circa 100,000 BCE - 3,200 BCE)
Let's start with the brief history of human education and knowledge sharing development.
So THE FIRST STOP of our journey is oral tradition. It was the only way of sharing knowledge about three thousand Before Christ's Era: In the earliest days of human civilization, information was transferred orally from generation to generation. Storytelling, folklore, and hands-on experiences were the primary and the only methods for education.
So, oral speech is reliable!






Birth of Writing and Numeracy
(circa 3,200 BCE)
Let's go on!

Around five thousand years ago, in ancient Mesopotamía, the first known writing system, cuneiform, was developed. This was closely tied with the invention of numbers and basic arithmetic, which allowed for record-keeping and administration of transactions, mostly in marketplaces.
First of all, there were needed convenient notations and the ability to accumulate knowledge. Fun thing to know about this piece of history is that the first forms of writing were about accounting and credits long precede the invention of money.
Well, clay tablets are reliable!
It is also interesting and quite well-known that cuneiform numerical notation was base sixty, unlike our base ten numbers, but they had a true place value system like we do, not like Roman numerals, for example. So the position of each digit within a number determines its value.





Scrolls and Libraries
(circa 1200 BCE — 15th Century CE)
Ok, now we move on to the third stage, which was connected with Scrolls and Libraries.
The accumulation of knowledge led to the establishment of libraries, such as the famous Library of Alexandria. Scrolls made of papyrus or parchment were the primary means of storing and sharing information.
Well, papyrus is reliable!

The Printing Press
(1440 CE)
The fourth stage was the invention of the printing press by Gutenberg. It revolutionized education, as we see today. Books became more accessible and affordable for everyone, leading to an increase in literacy rates and the dissemination of knowledge.
So, books are truly reliable!





Electricity and Telecommunication
(19th century)
With the advent of electricity, technologies like the telegraph and telephone emerged, allowing for real-time communication over long distances. This made the sharing of information quicker and more efficient.
The internet boom of the time was telegraph. It came into commercial use by 1840, submarine cables were laid by 1850. This allowed rapid communication between continents for the first time ever.

Digital Age and the Internet
(late 20th century)
Let's go on to the Digital Age and the Internet.
Here we are close to modern time: the second half of the 20th century saw the rise of computers and the Internet. Information became digitized, leading to the creation of search engines like Google, Yandex, Baidu, etc. This made accessing knowledge virtually instantaneous.
You know, the Internet is reliable!





Artificial Intelligence and ChatGPT
(21st century)
In the current era, AI technologies like ChatGPT have emerged, providing personalized and interactive learning experiences.
These technologies engage users in conversations, answer all your questions, and help in learning, taking the interaction with information to a new level. Here I need to emphasize that your language almost is not important for the system anymore, because it simply understands and translates any language to the internal representation!
But is ChatGPT reliable? We will discuss it later.

Information is extremely accessible!

The fact is that nowadays any information is extremely accessible! Even without an AI assistant, one has access to a massive amount of information.
For example, on YouTube, you can find an explanation for almost anything and to solve almost any problem, from repairing not working washing machine to executing the proper professional technique for a squat exercise, as we see here on slide.
Moreover, often, you can not only watch the video, but interact with the information, change some features and learn by exploration. With modern tools, you can even create similar materials yourself.
For instance, using Universe Sandbox application, you can literally make planet Mars collide with planet Venus and see the result. This simulates modern scientific theories about the world and displays all the physics of the process on your screen within a few minutes. Moreover, I am sure there is already, or definitely soon will be, a similar application for any scientific area.
And the next huge thing is that modern AI assistants not only search for facts and information, but they also generate it successfully. We might even say they generate a kind of new, original content. Partly, it is true even for these slides.
So we get an enormous amount of information, and the challenge is that we don't know at all whether it is true. Here we encounter a fundamental problem: is it possible to verify the answers of assistant, that might be smarter than we are =) Or maybe we can at least align it with our goals and work together effectively.

The Influence of ChatGPT in Education

Chegg Inc — an NYSE-listed company focused on improving learning and learning outcomes for students
Now let's move on to the second part of the talk and let's discuss the influence of ChatGPT in education.

We start with the CHEGG company, which focused on improving learning and learning outcomes for students. This company, as many others, was hit hard by the launch of ChatGPT.

In May 2023, the company said, “In the FIRST PART of the year, we saw no noticeable impact from ChatGPT on our new account growth, and we were meeting expectations on new sign-ups.”

But students that paid Chegg to practice exams and get homework feedback turned to ChatGPT instead. As a result, the company share price decreased more than 40%. In Chegg’s August 2023 earnings call, the company already said, “We’ve pivoted the company to harness AI to better serve learners.” Now they’re building internal LLM in partnership with Scale AI.

Pros and cons of ChatGPT in studying

Useful cases

  • helps answer questions for tools with poor or unreadable documentation (for example, it can tell you how to start using GDB)
  • gives good and quick examples for some JSONs, XMLs with the necessary characteristics, or some fixtures to fill the database
  • helps to overcome the fear of a “blank slate”


What it doesn't handle well

  • any task where you need to understand deeply the general context of the whole project
  • not typical problems in not so popular languages: if you ask to solve a dynamic programming problem in Haskell, it gives funny answers
  • for different mathematical facts about 50% of the GPT-generated proofs are incorrect
So what are the pros and cons of using ChatGPT in studying?

Let's begin with the USEFUL CASES.

It HELPS answer questions for tools with poor or inaccessible documentation (for example, it can tell you how to start using GNU Debugger).

It PROVIDES nice and quick examples for some JSONs and other objects, or some values to fill in the database.

ONE of the main points: it helps to overcome the fear of a “blank slate.”

And WHAT it doesn't handle well:

Definitely it is ANY TASK where you have a large general context of the whole project.

ALSO, it is non-typical problems in not so popular areas: if you ask to solve a dynamic programming problem in Haskell, it gives funny answers, which are totally wrong.

CRUCIAL for us: for different mathematical facts about 50% of the GPT-generated proofs are incorrect.

What is wrong with them?



  1. Intentionally, so as not to accidentally violate copyright
  2. AI is not reliable yet. If you believe it, damage may occur in almost everything related to information security, protocol development, infrastructure, etc.
  3. C++ requires human verification =)
  4. ChatGPT has problems with mathematical proofs
You may ask why not, what is wrong with them? Let's DELVE into understanding what the issues are.

FIRSTLY, there is a deliberate attempt to avoid copyright violation. This is an important consideration in today’s digital age.
NEXT, we must acknowledge that AI technology has not yet reached a point of complete reliability. This is crucial because over reliance on AI without proper verification can lead to significant issues in information security, protocol development, infrastructure, and other related domains.

THIRDLY, when it comes to programming languages such as C++ or Rust, human oversight is still required.

LASTLY, there are specific challenges that ChatGPT faces, such as difficulties in dealing with mathematical proofs.

This limitation highlights that while ChatGPT is a sophisticated tool, it is not without shortcomings in certain complex analytical tasks.

debugging problems in CODE or systems,

tasks related to SYSTEM engineering, including robotics,

and, importantly, engaging in HUMAN discussions and debates.
Each activity, in its own way, sharpens our ability to evaluate information critically and make reasoned judgments.

AI-powered cases from teachers

  • Create lecture outline $\to$ Make slides in reveal.js
  • Checking how easily an assignment task can be answered with the help of LLM
  • DALL-E 3 draws beautiful high-quality illustrations, in pencil or in vector formats

Let's talk about some of the practical applications of AI that teachers are utilizing today.

FIRSTLY, AI can significantly streamline the creation of lecture materials. With a simple command, AI can help you generate a detailed lecture outline and even convert that outline into visually engaging slides using tools like reveal.js. This is a great example of how AI can facilitate the preparation process for educators.

NEXT, teachers are experimenting with AI's capabilities by checking the effectiveness of language learning models in answering assignment questions. This not only tests the AI's understanding but also helps to identify how students might use such technology to aid their learning.

In the realm of ART and visuals, DALL-E 3 is another remarkable tool. It's capable of creating beautiful, high-quality illustrations, whether in pencil or vector formats. These illustrations can then be used to enhance teaching materials and engage students.

LASTLY, AI is taking on the task of handling routine responsibilities. From drafting course syllables to managing other repetitive tasks, AI is opening up space for teachers to focus on more personalized and creative aspects of their work. This shift is paving the way for a more efficient allocation of time and resources in educational environments.
