Oral Tradition
(circa 100,000 BCE - 3,200 BCE)
Let's start with the brief history of human education and knowledge sharing development.

1. Oral Tradition is about 3 thousand Before Christ's Birth: In the earliest days of human civilization, knowledge was transferred orally from generation to generation. Storytelling, folklore, and hands-on experiences were the primary methods for education.
So, oral speech is reliable!






Birth of Writing and Numeracy
(circa 3,200 BCE)
2. Birth of Writing and Numeracy (circa 3,200 BCE):

Around 5,200 years ago, in ancient Mesopotamia, the first known writing system, cuneiform, was developed. This was closely tied with the invention of numbers and basic arithmetic, which allowed for record-keeping and administration of transactions, mostly in marketplaces.
Naturally, then, first of all, there were needed convenient notations and the ability to accumulate knowledge. Fun things to know about this piece of history is that first forms of writing were about numbers and accounting and credits long precede the invention of money.
Well, clay tablets are reliable!
It is interesting and well-known, that cuneiform numerical notation was base sixty, unlike our base 10 numbers, but they had a true place value system like we do, not like Roman numerals for example.





Scrolls and Libraries
(circa 1200 BCE — 15th Century CE)
3. Scrolls and Libraries (circa 1200 BCE — 15th Century CE):
The accumulation of knowledge led to the establishment of libraries, such as the famous Library of Alexandria. Scrolls made of papyrus or parchment were the primary means of storing and sharing information.
Well, papyrus is reliable!

The Printing Press
(1440 CE)
4. The Printing Press (1440 CE):
The invention of the printing press by Gutenberg revolutionized education. Books became more accessible and affordable, leading to an increase in literacy rates and the dissemination of knowledge.
So, books are truly reliable!





Electricity and Telecommunication
(19th century)
5. Electricity and Telecommunication (19th Century CE):
With the advent of electricity, technologies like the telegraph and telephone emerged, allowing for real-time communication over long distances. This made the sharing of information quicker and more efficient.
The internet boom of the time was telegraph. It came into commercial use by 1840, submarine cables were laid by 1850. This allowed rapid communication between continents for the first time ever.

Digital Age and the Internet
(late 20th century)
6. Digital Age and the Internet (late 20th Century CE):
Now we are close to modern time: the latter half of the 20th century saw the rise of computers and the Internet. Information became digitized, leading to the creation of search engines like Google, Yandex, Baidu and etc. This made accessing knowledge virtually instantaneous.
You know, the Internet is reliable!





Artificial Intelligence and ChatGPT
(21st century)
7. Artificial Intelligence and ChatGPT (21st Century CE):
In the current era, AI technologies like ChatGPT have emerged, providing personalized and interactive learning experiences.
These technologies engage users in conversations, answer queries, and aid in learning, taking the interaction with information to a new level.
But is ChatGPT reliable? We will discuss it later.

Information is extremely accessible!

The fact is that nowadays any information is extremely accessile! Even without AI assistant one has massive amount of information.
For example, on Youtube you can find nice explanation of everything and to solve almost any problem from either repairing not working washing machine or how to perform the right professional technic of squat exercise.
Often you can not only watch the video, but interact with the information, to change some characteristics and to learn by exploration. With modern tools you can even create similar materials yourself.
For instance, using Universe Sandbox application, you can literally push Mars with Venus and look at the result, which will repeat modern scientific ideas about the world and show all the physics of the process in a few minutes on your screen. Moreover, I am sure there is or definitely soon will be similar application for any scientific area.
But the next huge thing is, that modern AI assistants not only search the facts and information, they generate it successfully. We might say it generates new original content.
So we get enormous amount of information and the challenge is, that we don't know if it is true or not. Here we got fundamental problem: is it possible to verify the system, that probably is smarter, then we are =) Maybe at least align it with our goals and work together effectively.

The Influence of ChatGPT in Education

Chegg Inc — an NYSE-listed company focused on improving learning and learning outcomes for students
Chegg, an NYSE-listed company focused on improving learning and learning outcomes for students, was hit hard by the launch of ChatGPT.
In May 2023, the company said “In the first part of the year, we saw no noticeable impact from ChatGPT on our new account growth and we were meeting expectations on new sign-ups.”
Students that paid Chegg to practice exams and get homework feedback turned to ChatGPT instead. As a result, their share price plummeted >40%. In Chegg’s August 2023 earnings call, the company said “We’ve pivoted the company to harness AI to better serve learners.” Now they’re building internal LLMs in partnership with Scale AI.

Pros and cons of ChatGPT

Useful cases:

  • helps answer questions for tools with poor or unreadable documentation (for example, it can tell you how to start using gdb)
  • gives good and quick examples for some JSONs, XMLs with the necessary characteristics, or some fixtures to fill the database
  • helps to overcome the fear of a “blank slate”


What it doesn't handle well:

  • any task where you need to understand deeply the generel context of the whole project
  • not typical problems in not so popular languages: if you ask to solve a dynamic programming problem in Haskell, it gives funny answers
  • for different math fact about half of the GPT-generated proofs are incorrect

AI-powered workflow for teaching

  • Direct the AI assistant step by step (as with human, yes)
  • Do a lot of checks: computations (via Wolfram for math or via compiler and tests for code)
  • Draw beautiful high-quality illustrations, in pencil or in vector formats
  • Send all routine tasks in a broad sense to the ChatGPT
